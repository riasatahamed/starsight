Data Sources & Attribution

Star PositionsGaia DR3 — ESA's billion-star catalog — for 131 stars (epoch 2016, propagated to J2000 with catalog proper motions), cross-matched via SIMBAD by Hipparcos number; the 72 very brightest stars (absent from Gaia DR3) use the Hipparcos-based Yale Bright Star Catalog, 5th rev. ed. Distances from Gaia DR3 parallaxes.
Star Names & SpectraIAU Catalog of Star Names (WGSN) with historical aliases resolved via SIMBAD. Visual magnitudes, color temperatures and luminosity classes from Yale BSC spectral types.
Deep Sky ObjectsOpenNGC — License-friendly NGC/IC database compiled from professional sources. Full Messier catalog (all 110) plus a curated subset of 96 notable NGC/IC objects and the Magellanic Clouds, with J2000 positions, magnitudes, sizes, and types.
Planets, Moon & SunAstronomy Engine by Don Cross — VSOP87-based ephemeris accurate to ±1 arcminute. Calculates positions, rise/set times, phases, and illumination for all solar system bodies.
Constellation DataIAU Constellation Boundaries (Delporte 1930) and Stellarium line data. 88 official constellations with accurate boundary polygons.
Weather & LocationOpen-Meteo API — free open-source weather data. No API key required. Licensed under CC BY 4.0.
NASA APODNASA Open APIs — Astronomy Picture of the Day. Public domain images unless noted otherwise.
All astronomical calculations use high-precision data sources. Star positions combine Gaia DR3 and the Hipparcos-based Yale BSC, propagated from J2000 with catalog proper motions. Planet positions use VSOP87 ephemeris via Astronomy Engine. Deep-sky objects from OpenNGC (full Messier + NGC/IC subset). Constellation boundaries follow IAU Delporte (1930) definitions. For research-grade astrometry, consult SIMBAD or VizieR.
